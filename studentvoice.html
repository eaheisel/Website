    Student Voice

    What is Student Voice?

    Student voice is the expression of a student in regards to anything related to education. Increasing student voice in the classroom empowers students and lets them know that their thoughts and opinions matter.

    Students can share their voice through text, audio, video, design, and so much more. Digital tools make it easier for students to have a voice since all students can respond to prompts, projects, etc. at the same time. Others can read, listen to, or watch studentsâ€™ responses and provide feedback, feedforward, and words of encouragement.

    Student Voice Tools

    The following is a list of my favorite Ed Tech Tools to increase student voice.

    Flipgrid

    Flipgrid is a tool that allows learners to record videos in response to a question or a prompt. It gives students a voice and a way to express themselves.

    Visit Flipgrid

    Padlet

    Padlet is another tool that gives students a voice. When learners post on a Padlet, they can respond using text, video, voice, and/or pictures. Learners can also collaborate with others through replies.

    Visit Padlet

    Google Forms

    Google Forms give learners the ability to respond to questions, reflect on their learning, and share their thoughts. When created using questions and prompts to encourage students to share their voice, the tool can be very effective and facilitators have many ways to view and share student responses.

    Visit Google Forms

    Mentimeter

    Mentimeter is a tool that allows the facilitator to ask their audience different questions in which their responses can be projected in multiple ways immediately for all to see. Word clouds and quizzes are just two of the thirteen question types. Students are able to share their opinions through the questions being asked.

    Visit Mentimeter
