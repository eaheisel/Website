    Instructional Coaching

    The job of an instructional coach encompasses four major roles: leading Professional Learning Communities (PLC), developing and providing professional development (PD), coaching cycles, and co-teach/team teaching. I empower teachers to use technology in innovative ways through each of my four responsibilities.

    Professional Learning Communities

    Each week, grade levels meet for a 80 minute PLC meeting. During these meetings we unpack standards, dive into data, plan lessons, model the use of ed tech tools, and plan for student engagement, empowerment, and creativity.

    Lesson and Unit Planning

    Before planning units and lessons, we unpack standards in our weekly PLCs. Unpacking the standards allows us to truly understand the learning target included in each of the standards. Once the learning targets have been identified and paced out within the unit, we begin to find the resources that can be used to teach the learning target. This process is modeled and facilitated with all grade levels.

    Additional support is provided to assist teachers in planning their small group instruction. Small group instruction in reading and math is planned for on a weekly basis. The plans are designed to meet the needs of the students in the group.

    Data Analysis

    Data analysis occurs shortly after data is collected through summative and formative assessments. Once the data is analyzed, we strategize as a PLC to come up with a plan to address the needs of the students - reteaching or enrichment. Then it is the responsibility of the teacher to act on that plan in order to teach and reassess students.

    As an instructional coach, I have created several tools that can be used for data analysis, strategizing, and acting on the plan. These include reflection documents, action plans, resources for reteaching, and standards based lesson plans.

    Modeling

    PLCs offer many opportunities to model best practices, high yield instructional strategies, student engagement strategies, and the use of ed tech tools.
